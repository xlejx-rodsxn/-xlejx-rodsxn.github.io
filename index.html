Welcome to my academic profile. Here I post my most recent publications and current work in the broad field of computational social sciences (CSS). My current affiliation is at University of Potsdam, where I study innovative methods to quantify irregular migration. In my research, I focus on the use of machine learning (ML) methods to inform research in the social sciences, often combining non-traditional data sources, as well as longitudinal and cross-sectional surveys. I am interested in both prediction and causal inference type of questions, as well as their fruitful combinations to explore, describe, and answer causal queries in social science research. My PhD dissertation in Sociology was on the topic of Family Behavior and Children's Wellbeing: Statistical Modeling and Measurement Issues at Humboldt Universität zu Berlin. I hold a MSc. Statistics and a one-year MA in Research Training in the Social Sciences both at Humboldt Universität zu Berlin. I hold from Universidad del Valle, Colombia.
